# Thesis Images

## Required Images

### 1. Honor Galileo and the Heroes of the Scientific Revolution
**Filename:** `galileo-heroes.jpg` or `galileo-heroes.png`

**Description:** A tribute to the pioneers of the Scientific Revolution

**Suggested Sources:**
- Search Wikimedia Commons for "Scientific Revolution heroes"
- Look for group portraits or commemorative artworks
- Alternative: Individual portraits arranged in a collage

**Dimensions:** Minimum 1000px width

---

### 2. Astronomer Copernicus, or Conversations with God
**Filename:** `copernicus-matejko-1873.jpg`

**Artist:** Jan Matejko  
**Year:** 1873

**Description:** This famous painting depicts Nicolaus Copernicus on a balcony at Frombork Castle, contemplating his revolutionary heliocentric model of the universe.

**Where to Find:**
- Wikimedia Commons: Search "Matejko Copernicus 1873"
- Direct link: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Matejko_Astronomer_Copernicus-Conversation_with_God.jpg
- This is a public domain image

**Dimensions:** Original painting is quite large, download high resolution

**Notes:** 
- This is one of Matejko's most famous works
- Shows Copernicus with astronomical instruments
- Symbolizes the connection between science and divine inspiration
- Public domain - free to use

---

## To Add Images:

1. Download images from sources above
2. Save in this directory (`images/thesis/`)
3. Update `thesis.html` to replace placeholders:

Replace this:
```html
<div style="background: #F4E8D0; border: 3px solid #D4AF37; padding: 3rem...">
```

With this:
```html
<img src="images/thesis/copernicus-matejko-1873.jpg" 
     alt="Astronomer Copernicus, or Conversations with God by Jan Matejko, 1873"
     style="width: 100%; height: auto; border: 3px solid #D4AF37; border-radius: 5px;">
```
